# Production & Upgrades Manual

Welcome to the industrial heart of BusinessCraft! This guide explains how your town produces resources, manages its population, and advances through the technological ages.

## 1. The Town Core
Your Town Hall is the brain of your settlement. It tracks:
- **Resources**: Wood, Stone, Food, Iron, etc. These are virtual "Resource Units" stored in the town's data.
- **Population**: The number of villagers living and working in your town.
- **Happiness**: How satisfied your population is. Low happiness stops production!

## 2. Production System
Your town automatically runs "Production Cycles" to generate resources.

### How it Works
1.  **Recipes**: Your town knows certain recipes (e.g., "Basic Farming" turns nothing into Wheat).
2.  **Cycles**: Each recipe takes a certain amount of time (e.g., 5 days).
3.  **Inputs & Outputs**: Most recipes require inputs (e.g., "Smelting" needs Iron Ore + Coal) to produce outputs (Iron Ingots).
4.  **Automatic Processing**: If you have the inputs and space for the outputs, the cycle runs automatically.

### Constraints
-   **Storage Caps**: You have a limit on how much of each resource you can store. If you hit the cap, production stops ("Stalled").
-   **Happiness**: If happiness drops below 50%, workers strike and production halts.
-   **Inputs**: You must have enough input resources to start a cycle.

## 3. Upgrades & Research
Unlock new capabilities and improve your town's efficiency through the Upgrade Tree.

### Researching
-   Your town automatically generates **Research Points** ( RP) over time.
-   Select an upgrade in the **Upgrades Tab** of the Town Interface.
-   Once the RP cost is met, the upgrade unlocks!

### Benefits of Upgrades
-   **New Recipes**: Unlock "Advanced Smelting", "Bakery", etc.
-   **Passive Effects**:
    -   **Speed Boosts**: Make specific recipes run faster (e.g., "+20% Farming Speed").
    -   **Cap Increases**: Store more resources (e.g., "+500 Food Storage").
    -   **Population Cap**: Allow more villagers to live in your town.

## 4. Population & Happiness
Your people power your economy.

-   **Population**: Grows automatically if you have **Housing Space** (from Upgrades) and **Food**.
-   **Food Consumption**: Your population eats food every day. If you run out of food, happiness plummets.
-   **Happiness Factors**:
    -   **Food Security**: Having a steady supply of food.
    -   **Luxury Goods**: Higher-tier resources (like Bread vs. raw Wheat) boost happiness more.
    -   **Overcrowding**: Too many people and not enough upgrades can lower happiness.

## 5. Tips for Mayors
-   **Rush Farming**: Food is life. Upgrade your farming tech early to support a growing population.
-   **Watch Storage**: If your storage fills up, your production lines stop. Use the **Contract Board** (see Economy Manual) to sell excess goods.
-   **Balance Growth**: Don't upgrade population capacity if you can't feed the new mouths!
